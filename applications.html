Microwave Communication Applications

Terrestrial Microwave Links

Terrestrial microwave systems are widely used where laying optical fiber or copper cable is difficult, expensive, or impossible. They work on Line-of-Sight principle using tall towers and repeater stations.

  • Cellular Network Backhaul: Connecting mobile towers to core network.
  • Mountain & Rural Connectivity: Linking remote villages and hill stations.
  • Disaster Recovery Links: Quick deployment after earthquakes and floods.
  • Campus & City Networks: Wireless bridging between buildings.

Satellite Microwave Systems

Satellite communication is used when extremely long distances or very large coverage areas are required. It works using uplink and downlink between earth station and satellite.

  • TV & Radio Broadcasting: DTH television, live event broadcasting.
  • GPS & Navigation Systems: Positioning and timing services.
  • Weather Forecasting: Data collection from space sensors.
  • International Communication: Intercontinental voice and data links.

Infrared (IR) Communication Applications

Consumer & Embedded Systems

Infrared is mainly used for short-range, secure, indoor communication because it cannot penetrate walls and does not cause interference to nearby rooms.

  • TV & AC Remote Controls
  • Home Automation Systems
  • Motion Sensors & Security Systems
  • Industrial Remote Controllers

Computer & Office Networks

Infrared is used in closed environments where high security and zero cross-room interference is required.

  • Wireless Mouse & Keyboard
  • Short-Range Device Synchronization
  • Secure Conference Room Data Links
  • Medical Equipment Communication
